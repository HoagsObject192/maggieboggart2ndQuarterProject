Cinnamon Rolls


Ingredients

Dough

  • 1 cup milk
  • ½ cup butter
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 6 cups all-purpose flour

Filling

  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • 2 cups dark brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon

Frosting

  • 2 cups confectioners’ sugar
  • 3 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tablespoon butter, softened
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 tablespoons milk

Description

Cinnamon rolls originated in Northern Europe, particularly Sweden, where kanelbullar became a traditional sweet bread enjoyed with coffee. They have a warm, comforting flavor profile dominated by aromatic cinnamon, rich butter, and soft yeasty dough, often balanced by a hint of vanilla or creaminess from icing. The taste is sweet and spiced, with a tender, fluffy interior and a slightly caramelized, sticky swirl that makes each bite cozy and indulgent.


Procedure

    • Warm the milk and melt the butter together. Add water and let cool until lukewarm.
    • Mix yeast, sugar, salt, eggs, and flour into the liquid mixture to form a dough.
    • Knead the dough on a floured surface until smooth and elastic.
    • Divide the dough in half and roll each portion into a rectangle.
    • Spread softened butter over the dough.
    • Mix brown sugar and cinnamon, then sprinkle evenly over the buttered dough.
    • Roll the dough tightly, seal the edges, and slice into rolls.
    • Place rolls into greased baking pans and let rise.
    • Bake in a preheated oven until golden brown.
    • Mix frosting ingredients until smooth.
    • Spread frosting over warm cinnamon rolls and serve.
